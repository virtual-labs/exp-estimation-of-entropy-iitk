<b>Steps:</b><br>
1. Hover over the instruments to understand their functions, then click Start to begin the experiment.<br>
2. Choose one of the following options:<br>
• Standardize Your HCl Solution → Click Standardization of HCl<br>
• Directly Jump to Main Experiment → Click Main Experiment<br><br>

<b>Standardization of HCl: </b><br>
3. Click on the flask to pour 5 ml Na₂CO₃ into the measuring cylinder.<br>
4. Click on the measuring cylinder to transfer 5 ml Na₂CO₃ into the conical flask.<br>
5. Click on the micropipette to collect 3 drops of Bromo Cresol Green indicator.<br>
6. Click again on the micropipette to add the indicator to the flask.<br>
7. Click on the flask to start titration.<br>
8. Shake the flask and add HCl drop by drop until the solution changes from blue to yellow.<br>
9. Click Next to proceed to the main experiment.<br><br>

<b>Main Experiment: </b><br>
10. Click on the borax powder plate to add 25 g of borax into the flask.<br>
11. Click on the water beaker to pour 75 ml distilled water.<br>
12. Click on the measuring cylinder to transfer that 75 ml distilled water into the flask.<br>
13. Click on the flask to mix the solution for 10–15 minutes.<br>
14. Place the flask in the temperature bath, set the required temperature, and wait 10 minutes.<br>
15. Collect 5 ml borax solution using the micropipette and pour it into fresh and clean flask.<br>
16. Add Bromo Cresol Green indicator using the micropipette.<br>
17. Click on the flask to start titration.<br>
18. Shake the flask and add standard HCl until the solution turns yellow.<br>
19. Repeat the experiment for other temperatures, starting again from Step 10.<br>
